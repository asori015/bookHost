THE PLAYER OF GAMES
Iain M. Banks
In Consider Phlebas, Iain Banks created a universe,
two civilisations and a cosmic war. Now he has
written a witty, wide-screen successor, set several
hundred years later . . .
 
 
The Culture - a human/machine symbiotic society - has
thrown up many great Game Players, and one of the
greatest is Gurgeh. Jernau Morat Gurgeh. The Player of
Games. Master of every board, computer and strategy.
Bored with success, Gurgeh travels to the Empire of
Azad, cruel and incredibly wealthy, to try their fabulous
game . . . a game so complex, so like life itself, that the
winner becomes emperor. Mocked, blackmailed, almost
murdered, Gurgeh accepts the game, and with it the
challenge of his life - and very possibly his death.
